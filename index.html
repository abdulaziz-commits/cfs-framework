CFS Framework

Flow and Constraint

Outcomes are not decided —
they are formed.
Most systems are managed as if outcomes can be controlled directly.

In reality, systems evolve through flow, accumulate pressure, and become shaped by constraints until outcomes emerge.

This framework offers a different perspective:
Understanding how systems actually work — not how we assume they do.
Why this matters:

Projects delay despite effort.
Pressure increases without progress.
The same problems repeat.

These are not random issues.
They are structural.
By Abdulaziz bin Saeed Al-Wahaibi
